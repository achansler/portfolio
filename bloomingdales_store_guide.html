Bloomingdale's

In-Store Guide

Role

Research, UX, UI

Description

The Bloomingdale’s In-Store Guide is a feature on the app that helps shoppers navigate through the large and constant changing stores. The feature geo-locates a user, and launches when they’re in a Bloomingdale’s store.

Research

Sales Associate Interviews

To understand pain points that could be solved with the guide, I interviewed sales associates at the largest stores. The sales associates spoke about the most common questions they get from shoppers.

“Where can I find this designer?”

“Do you have this in my size?”

“Is this available in red?”

“Do you carry this designer?”

“Do you carry this product?”

Shopper Interviews

I interviewed shoppers to understand the most common problems when shopping in a Bloomingdale’s store.

“These stores are huge. It’s hard to find what I’m looking for.”

“It’s sometimes hard to find someone to ask basic questions.”

Wireframes

When a user enters a store, the app would inform them of the in-store guide, explain the benefits, and direct them to use it. This screen would only appear once as a feature announcement.

To elevate the ability to search, I created two options to test. The first was a large search page that allowed a user to input information in a conversational form. The second followed the navtive iOS search pattern.

Second flow option

To find in-store events, users can search for dates as well as save an event by tapping on the heart icon.

If a user has a specific question about a product, they can scan the barcode on a tag and see information about colors, sizes, and see product reviews. Users can also easily access their saved items.

Usability Testing

During usability testing, I learned that the native iOS search bar was a more intuitive experience. I also learned that users often search their email to look for their loyalty information to get points when purchasing an item, so I designed a screen with a users loyalty information.
